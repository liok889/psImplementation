Bivariate correlation stimuli

Generate bivariate data at a chosen sample Pearson correlation (the exact-correlation method used by Rensink & Baldridge and Harrison et al.), render it as a scatterplot or parallel coordinates, and export a 256×256 PNG to feed through the Portilla–Simoncelli pipeline.
The exported PNG omits axes — just the data marks on white — so the PS analysis sees the data layout as texture. Positive correlation makes parallel-coordinate lines run roughly parallel; negative correlation makes them cross (an X pattern).
